Dahl Makhani

Description

Dahl Makhani is a butter dahl and is my favourite dahl recipe. Easy to make and good for batch cooking.

Ingredients

    T
  • To Pressure Cook 3/4 cup urad dal sabut (whole black lentil) 165 grams 1/4 cup rajma (red kidney beans) 60 grams 1 teaspoon salt 3.5 cups water 28 oz
    • Masala for the Dal
  • 1 tablespoon ghee
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1 medium white onion 115 grams, finely grated
  • 2 teaspoons ginger garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup tomato puree
  • 1/2 teaspoon kashmiri red chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt or to taste
  • 1.5 cups water 12 oz, as needed
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 cup cream 60 ml

Method

  1. Wash and rinse urad dal (whole black lentil) and rajma (kidney beans) in a large bowl. Soak in 3 cups water overnight.
  2. Drain the water in which the dal and rajma was soaked. Transfer the dal and rajma to a pressure cooker with 1 teaspoon salt. Add around 3.5 cups water.
  3. Pressure cook on high-medium heat for 10 minutes, then lower the heat to low-medium and cook for another 10 minutes. In total around 15 to 20 minutes.
  4. Mash some of the dal and rajma using a potato masher. Then turn on the heat to lowest heat and let the dal simmer while you make the masala.
  5. To make the masala, in a large pot/pan, heat 2 tablespoons butter. Cook the onion for around 6 to 7 minutes or until it turns light golden brown. Keep stirring it continuously so that it doesn't burn and keep heat on medium.
  6. Add the ginger garlic paste and cook for 1 to 2 minute until the raw smell goes away.
  7. Add the tomato puree and mix. Cook for 2 minutes or until the puree mixes well with masala and oil starts oozing out from the sides.
  8. Add in the boiled dal (which had been simmering for around 10 to 15 mins you were making the masala) and mix.
  9. Add garam masala, kashmiri red chili powder and salt. Mix to combine.
  10. Add 1/2 cup water, stir and set heat to low. Let it simmer on low heat uncovered for around 45 minutes.
  11. Add sugar and mix after the dal has simmered for 45 minutes.
  12. Add the remaining 1 tablespoon butter and 1/4 cup cream. Mix well.
  13. Simmer for 10 more minutes on low heat after adding the cream. Dal will become really creamy by now.
